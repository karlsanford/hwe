Very rarely are technologies issues 100% technology.

Buying a CRM application won’t get you more customers. Having a slick email client won’t make you more productive. Technology is merely a tool. Without proper fitness of purpose and training / motivation of users, it rarely lives up to the claims of its marketers.

Very rarely can business processes be optimized without technology.

What is an organization? At the basic level it is a collection of individuals working together toward roughly the same goal. Each one of these individual has a unique skillset which allows them to contribute value to the organization. In order to work together effectively, they need to be able to create, view, and share information. THIS is what technology is good at.

People are not committed to the ideals of other people for their own lives

Change is hard. Change sucks. Change requires me to take my brain of auto pilot. Whether they express it verbally or not, these are the feelings and emotions people have about change. Typically this is the hardest part of improvement and every organization is unique in how it should be handled. One size does not fit all.

What we need isn’t a perfect plan… What we need is perfect EXECUTION.

If we all had a nickel for every great idea we’ve had, we’d all be on a beach somewhere… Turning ideas into reality is not easy, sometimes it even seems impossible. Sometimes you don’t even know where to start, much less where to finish. You need to be able to have some sort of roadmap to guide your decision making, but no plan in the world has true value without implementation.

Conclusion

People, Process, Technology, and Implementation is the fundamental recipe and just like your grandma’s old family recipe, miss one thing and it just doesn’t work.
